About This Procedure

What are Porcelain Veneers?

Porcelain veneers are ultra-thin ceramic shells custom-crafted to fit over the front surface of your teeth. They correct discolouration, chips, gaps, mild crowding, and irregularities — delivering a uniform, natural-looking smile designed specifically for your face.

Shanghai's dental clinics use high-grade ceramic and digital smile design technology to produce veneers that match your facial features and aesthetic preferences with precision. The lab work is completed to exacting standards within your trip timeframe.

Vday coordinates your dental consultations, treatment schedule, and any between-visit time — so your entire smile transformation fits within a single well-planned trip.

What to Expect

The Procedure Process

Your first visit involves a full smile assessment and digital design preview. A minimal amount of enamel is removed from the front of each tooth before temporary veneers are placed while your permanent ones are crafted in the lab.

At your second visit (typically 5–7 days later), your permanent porcelain veneers are bonded in place. Final adjustments ensure a perfect fit and bite. Results are immediate — you leave with your new smile.

Common Questions

Veneers FAQ

Do porcelain veneers damage your teeth?
A minimal amount of enamel (typically 0.3–0.5mm) is removed to allow the veneer to sit flush. This is a permanent, irreversible process, so the decision should be considered carefully with your dentist.
How many teeth can be veneered?
Most clients veneer 6–10 upper front teeth for a balanced smile transformation. Your dentist will recommend the ideal number based on your goals and facial proportions.
How long do porcelain veneers last?
With proper care, porcelain veneers last 10–15 years. Avoiding biting hard objects, wearing a night guard if you grind, and maintaining good oral hygiene significantly extend their lifespan.
Is the veneer procedure painful?
Teeth preparation is done under local anaesthesia. Some sensitivity is normal for a few days after preparation and placement, which subsides quickly.
