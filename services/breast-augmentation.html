What is Breast Augmentation?

Breast augmentation uses implants — or in some cases fat transfer — to increase breast size, restore volume lost after weight change or pregnancy, or improve symmetry. The procedure is performed under general anaesthesia, with implants placed beneath the breast tissue or chest muscle depending on your anatomy and desired outcome.

Shanghai's plastic surgeons have extensive experience across a wide range of implant profiles and placement techniques, known for proportionate, natural-feeling results tailored to each client's frame and aesthetic goals.

The Procedure Process

During consultation, your surgeon will discuss implant type, size, profile, and placement — subglandular or submuscular — based on your anatomy and goals. Surgery is performed under general anaesthesia and typically takes 1–2 hours, after which a surgical support bra is worn for the first 6 weeks.

Most clients are comfortable within 2–3 days and return to light activity within 1–2 weeks, with strenuous exercise resuming at 4–6 weeks. The final shape settles fully at 3–6 months as the implants drop and soften into place.

Breast Augmentation FAQ

Silicone gel implants are the most commonly used for a natural feel. Round and anatomical (teardrop) profiles are both available. Your surgeon will recommend the best option based on your body type, existing anatomy, and desired outcome.

Most clients are mobile within 2–3 days. Light work can be resumed in 1–2 weeks, while strenuous activity and heavy lifting should be avoided for 4–6 weeks. A surgical support bra is worn for the first 6 weeks.

Implants are long-lasting but are not guaranteed to last a lifetime. Replacement may be considered after 10–20 years, or sooner if any changes occur. Your surgeon will advise on monitoring and long-term care.

Incisions are typically placed in the inframammary fold — the crease beneath the breast — keeping scarring well concealed. Scars fade significantly over 12–18 months.
