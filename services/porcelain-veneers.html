What are Porcelain Veneers?

Porcelain veneers are ultra-thin ceramic shells custom-crafted to fit over the front surface of your teeth. They correct discolouration, chips, gaps, mild crowding, and irregular shapes — delivering a uniform, natural-looking smile designed specifically for your face.

Shanghai's dental clinics use high-grade ceramic and digital smile design technology to produce veneers that match your facial features and aesthetic preferences with precision. Vday coordinates your consultations, treatment schedule, and any between-visit time, so your entire transformation fits within a single well-planned trip.

The Procedure Process

Your dentist begins with a digital smile design preview, photography, and full treatment planning. Minimal enamel reduction is then performed under local anaesthesia, with temporary veneers placed while your permanent set is crafted in the clinic's lab over 5–7 days.

Once ready, your permanent veneers are bonded in place, with final colour, shape, and bite adjustments made for a perfect finish. The result is immediate — you leave Shanghai with your complete smile transformation.

Porcelain Veneers FAQ

A minimal amount of enamel — typically 0.3–0.5mm — is removed to allow the veneer to sit flush against the tooth. This is a permanent, irreversible process, so the decision should be made carefully together with your dentist.

Most clients veneer 6–10 upper front teeth for a balanced smile transformation, though full upper and lower sets are also common. Your dentist will recommend the ideal number based on your goals and facial proportions.

With proper care, porcelain veneers last 10–15 years. Avoiding biting hard objects, wearing a night guard if you grind your teeth, and maintaining good oral hygiene significantly extends their lifespan.

Tooth preparation is done under local anaesthesia, so you won't feel pain during the procedure. Some mild sensitivity is normal for a few days afterward and subsides quickly.
