Porcelain Veneers Cost: Singapore, UK & Australia vs Shanghai

Porcelain veneers are a significant dental investment wherever you have them done. But the cost difference between Singapore, the UK, Australia, and Shanghai is substantial — enough that a growing number of clients from each of those regions choose to travel. Here is an honest breakdown of what drives the gap, and what you actually get.

By Vday Aesthetic Concierge 7 min read April 2026

What Do Porcelain Veneers Cost in Singapore?

At reputable private dental clinics in Singapore, porcelain veneers typically cost SGD $1,200–2,000 per tooth. A standard 8-tooth smile transformation runs between SGD $9,600 and $16,000 — and that's before consultation fees, temporaries, or any supplementary treatment.

Singapore dental pricing reflects real costs: some of the highest commercial rents in Asia, expensive regulatory compliance, and a smaller patient volume per dentist compared to high-volume markets. The cost is not inflated — it reflects the environment in which those clinics operate. But it does mean that the procedure is out of reach for many, or that clients are pushed toward composite veneers as a cheaper alternative.

Medisave and insurance rarely cover elective cosmetic dentistry in Singapore, so the full cost is usually paid out of pocket.

What Do Porcelain Veneers Cost in the UK?

In the United Kingdom, porcelain veneers at private dental practices typically run £600–1,200 per tooth (approximately SGD $1,050–2,100). An 8-veneer smile transformation costs roughly £4,800–9,600 (SGD $8,400–16,800). London practices and high-demand cosmetic clinics frequently quote at the top of this range and beyond.

NHS dental care does not cover elective veneers. UK clients who want a quality cosmetic result go through private dentistry, and the pricing reflects the high cost of running private dental practices in British cities — staffing, premises, indemnity insurance, and lab costs are all significant.

Travel from the UK to Shanghai for a veneer trip adds approximately SGD $1,200–1,800 return per person in flights and accommodation. On an 8-veneer procedure, the total cost including travel is still meaningfully lower than a UK private clinic — often by 40–60%.

What Do Porcelain Veneers Cost in Australia?

Australian cosmetic dental fees are among the highest in the world. Porcelain veneers at reputable practices typically cost AUD $1,800–3,000 per tooth (approximately SGD $1,600–2,700). An 8-veneer case can reach AUD $14,400–24,000 (SGD $13,000–21,600).

Australia's high cost reflects its high labour costs, strong regulatory framework, and the relatively low density of specialist cosmetic dentists relative to demand. Private health insurance funds partial rebates on some dental procedures, but cosmetic veneers are generally excluded. Australian clients travelling to Shanghai for veneers save substantially even after factoring in flights, typically 50–65% compared to home.

What Do Porcelain Veneers Cost in Shanghai?

At Vday's partner clinics in Shanghai, porcelain veneers are priced at approximately SGD $400–700 per tooth, depending on the ceramic material specified and the number of teeth. An 8-veneer treatment typically costs SGD $3,200–5,600 all-in, including temporaries and bonding.

This is not a budget or discounted service. The lower price reflects structural differences in the Shanghai market: clinics operate at significantly higher volume (which builds both skill and efficiency), lab costs are lower due to local ceramist availability, and commercial overheads are a fraction of those in Singapore, London, or Sydney. The ceramic materials — IPS e.max lithium disilicate, the same used at premium clinics worldwide — are the same.

Is the Quality the Same?

This is the most important question — and the honest answer is yes, when you choose the right clinic. The ceramic material most widely used for premium porcelain veneers globally — Ivoclar Vivadent IPS e.max lithium disilicate — is manufactured in Liechtenstein and distributed worldwide. It is the same product in Shanghai as in Singapore, London, or Melbourne.

What varies between clinics is not the material but the skill of the dentist and ceramist, the quality of the impression or scan, and the care taken with colour matching and bite adjustment. Vday's partner clinics are selected based on their track record with international clients, their ceramics quality, and their digital imaging capability. We do not work with clinics that cut corners on materials or lab work.

High volume in Shanghai's cosmetic dental market also means that dentists working exclusively in smile design and veneers develop a level of procedural speed and consistency that is genuinely hard to match in lower-volume Western markets. Experience built over thousands of cases creates precision.

Hidden Costs to Watch For

Whether you're getting veneers in Singapore, the UK, Australia, or Shanghai, always clarify these before agreeing to a quote:

  • Consultation fee — some clinics charge SGD $100–300 for the initial smile design consultation; Vday's partner clinics include this in the overall treatment cost
  • Temporaries — ask whether temporary veneers are included; they typically are at reputable clinics
  • Post-bond adjustments — minor bite adjustments after bonding should be included; confirm this upfront
  • Number of teeth in the quote — get the exact count clear. Some clinics quote for 6 teeth and you discover you need 8 for a balanced result
  • GST or VAT — in Singapore, dental fees attract 9% GST. In the UK, cosmetic dentistry is subject to 20% VAT. Confirm whether quoted prices are inclusive or exclusive of tax

When Is Going to Shanghai Worth It?

The break-even point is roughly 4 veneers. At that number, the savings from Shanghai versus Singapore typically cover flights and 7–10 nights in a mid-range hotel, with meaningful savings remaining. At 8 veneers — the most common full-smile treatment — clients from Singapore save SGD $6,000–10,000 net of travel. Clients from the UK and Australia save even more.

The trip itself is a significant factor for many clients. Shanghai is a fascinating city — well connected, with excellent food, infrastructure, and accommodation across all budgets. The 7–10 day window between veneer appointments is time many clients genuinely enjoy, rather than a logistical inconvenience.
