FUE Cost: Singapore vs Shanghai — An Honest Comparison

FUE in Singapore can cost SGD $8,000 to $35,000 or more depending on graft count and clinic. The same procedure in Shanghai — at a clinic with equivalent or higher surgical volume — typically costs 40–60% less. Here's why, and what you need to know before deciding.

6 min read·April 2026

What FUE Costs in Singapore

Singapore hair transplant clinics typically price FUE on a per-graft basis, ranging from SGD $3 to $10 per graft depending on the surgeon's seniority, clinic prestige, and number of grafts required — with premium clinics charging up to SGD $11 per graft inclusive of GST. A moderate case of 2,500 grafts could cost SGD $7,500–$25,000. Advanced cases requiring 4,000+ grafts can exceed SGD $35,000.

These prices reflect Singapore's high commercial rents, staffing costs, and a relatively small pool of hair transplant specialists. The demand-supply ratio keeps prices elevated.

What FUE Costs in Shanghai

Shanghai's leading aesthetic hospitals price FUE significantly lower — typically SGD $1.50 to $4 per graft, all-in. The same 2,500-graft case that costs $15,000 in Singapore might cost $4,500–$8,000 in Shanghai, including surgeon fees, facility charges, and medications.

Why Is Shanghai So Much Cheaper?

Lower cost does not mean lower quality. The price difference is structural, not quality-related:

  • Volume: Shanghai's top hair transplant centres perform hundreds of procedures monthly. This volume drives surgeon expertise and operational efficiency — costs spread across more cases.
  • Overheads: Commercial property, labour, and operating costs in Shanghai are substantially lower than Singapore.
  • Competition: A larger pool of qualified specialists creates a more competitive market that keeps prices honest.
  • Healthcare infrastructure: China's scale of medical training produces world-class specialists at lower labour cost.

The True Cost: Including Travel

You'll need to factor in flights and accommodation. A return flight from Singapore to Shanghai typically costs SGD $300–$600. Budget 5–7 nights in Shanghai (days 0–5 post-surgery) at SGD $80–$200/night depending on your preference.

Adding SGD $1,000–$2,000 for travel costs, the all-in cost of Shanghai FUE is still significantly below Singapore pricing for equivalent procedures. For clients serious about the decision, the maths make sense.

Who Should Consider Going Overseas?

Medical travel for aesthetic procedures has grown steadily since 2020. It suits clients who:

  • Have done thorough research and are confident in their decision
  • Are comfortable communicating via a concierge who handles translation
  • Can take 5–7 days away from work for the procedure and initial recovery
  • Want to maximise graft count and quality within their budget

It is less suitable for clients who need very frequent in-person follow-up or have complex medical histories requiring ongoing local specialist care.
