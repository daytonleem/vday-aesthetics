Eyelid Surgery Cost: Singapore vs Shanghai — Is It Worth Flying?

Blepharoplasty in Singapore is accessible — but it carries a significant price premium over the same procedure in Shanghai. Here is a transparent breakdown of what each market charges, what is included, and how to calculate whether the trip makes financial sense.

By Vday Aesthetic Concierge 6 min read May 2026

What Eyelid Surgery Costs in Singapore

Singapore's aesthetic surgery market is well-developed, with experienced surgeons operating in accredited facilities. The pricing reflects high overheads — clinic rental, GST, staffing, and Singapore's generally elevated cost structure.

Both Singapore and Shanghai pricing typically covers:

  • Anaesthesia (local with sedation, or general)
  • Operating facility and nursing care
  • Post-operative medication

Why Shanghai Surgeons Have a Specific Edge

Cost savings alone do not justify flying for surgery — quality has to be equivalent or better. For eyelid surgery specifically, Shanghai has a credible argument for being the better choice, not just the cheaper one.

Shanghai surgeons perform blepharoplasty on Asian patients almost exclusively, at extremely high volume. Many of the country's most cited techniques for Asian double eyelid correction were developed and refined in Shanghai and Beijing. The anatomical familiarity that comes from operating on thousands of Asian eyelids per year is not easily replicated in a lower-volume market.

Singapore has excellent surgeons, but the broader pool includes many who trained primarily on Caucasian anatomy. When it comes to a procedure where subtle anatomical understanding makes the difference between a natural result and an obvious one, specialisation matters significantly.

What About Malaysian Clients?

Malaysian clients typically find the cost equation even more compelling. Kuala Lumpur's eyelid surgery market is priced lower than Singapore's, but Shanghai still offers meaningful savings — and the flight from KL to Shanghai is comparable in cost and duration to the Singapore–Shanghai route.

Is It Worth It?

For most clients considering upper and lower blepharoplasty combined, the answer is yes — the savings comfortably cover the trip, and you receive the procedure from surgeons with specialist expertise in Asian eyelid anatomy.

For upper eyelid only (the lower end of the Singapore price range), the equation is closer. Some clients find it worth it for the surgical expertise alone; others prefer the convenience of staying local for a simpler procedure.
