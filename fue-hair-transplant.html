About This Procedure

What is FUE Hair Transplant?

Follicular Unit Extraction (FUE) is the most advanced hair restoration technique available today. Individual hair follicles are extracted from the donor area and precisely implanted into thinning or balding areas — with no linear scar and minimal downtime.

Shanghai's leading clinics perform FUE at exceptional volume, giving their surgeons a level of precision and speed that is difficult to match elsewhere. Costs are significantly lower than Singapore, Australia or the UK — without any compromise on quality.

Common Questions

FUE FAQ

Is FUE hair transplant permanent?
Yes. FUE is considered a permanent solution because the transplanted follicles are taken from areas resistant to hair loss. However, existing non-transplanted hair may continue to thin over time, which is why some clients opt for a second session in the future.
Does it hurt?
The procedure is performed under local anaesthesia — you feel no pain during the session. Mild soreness and tightness is normal for a few days after.
How many grafts do I need for a hair transplant?
The number of grafts depends on your hair loss severity, density goals, and donor area. Mild recession may require 1,500–2,000 grafts, while more advanced cases can need 3,000–5,000 or more. A proper assessment is needed for an accurate estimate.
Why do it in Shanghai?
Shanghai clinics perform FUE at very high volume, meaning surgeons develop exceptional speed and precision. Costs are significantly lower than Singapore or the UK — without compromising on quality.
How soon can I fly back after the procedure?
Most clients fly home 3–5 days after surgery. We recommend staying at least one night for a post-procedure check before travel. Long-haul flights are generally comfortable as the scalp is no longer sensitive by day 3.
